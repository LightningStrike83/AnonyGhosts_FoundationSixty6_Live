Our Network

Foundation Sixty6 is made of people who strive to make a difference and connect youth with the help that they need.

We are determined to continue expanding our network of counsellors, physicians, doctors, and volunteers that care about mental health and want to make a difference in the lives of youth and families facing mental health challenges.

Why Become Part Of Our Network?

Foundation Sixty6 is built on the heart and soul of people who care about mental health and want to provide a safe and secure future for struggling youth and their families. We are the people who want to make a difference in the world, because the cost of inaction is too great.

  • As of 2019, about 4000 Canadians die from suicide each year.
  • In 2018, 53% of suicides accounted for youth between the ages of 15 and 24
  • 14% of high-school students have seriously contemplated suicide
  • Indigenous youth die by suicide 6 times more often than non-Indigineous youth
  • Youth are resorting to self-diagnosis, and often times they are misinformed and cannot properly help themselves
  • Opportunities for stability disappear due to misinformation or misconceptions

Here in Ontario, we are facing major gaps that impact and impair those seeking mental health services.

  • As a society, we are facing a mental health epidemic, especially post-COVID
  • There are major gaps in Ontario's healthcare system, and getting help is more difficult than it should be
  • There is an inadequate number of doctors and healthcare providers who can provide those services as well
  • Struggling youth without substance use disorder (SUD) are deprioritized
  • When budget decisions are made, mental healthcare is not a priority

We at Foundation Sixty6 want to help bridge these gaps. We want to expand our network and provide support to struggling youth. We aim to make a difference in the world, and we encourage you to support and join our cause.

Our Goals

We are always looking to expand our network, but we also have a plan of action on how to use our network.

  • Primary Goal:Facilitate access to better mental health services for youth 16-25 who suffer from sever mental health conditions under the umbrella of Psychosis, BPD, and Schizophrenia
  • Build a network of healthcare providers: We want to connect patients to HCPs who are willing to support affordable access to mental health care.
  • Build a network of companies and employers: We want to connect youth to employers who understand the struggles and are willing to provide flexible and accommodating employment opportunities.
  • Identify fundraising initiatives: We want to have a network that can provide sustainability and long-term support for Foundation Sixty6.
